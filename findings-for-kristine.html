📜 Historical Context (Not on Any Tree)

🏛️ Captain Simon Tamondong (1802) ⚠️ 75% confidence
Gobernadorcillo (highest local position) of San Carlos City in 1802. Oversaw reconstruction of the Minor Basilica of St. Dominic after devastating earthquakes. Part of the principalía (ruling class). Likely ancestor of Juan Tamondong (1850) — exact link unproven.
📄 Wikipedia: Minor Basilica of St. Dominic, San Carlos
🏛️ Kingdom of Caboloan ✅ Academic
San Carlos City (originally Binalatongan) was the capital of the pre-colonial Kingdom of Caboloan — a sovereign Filipino polity with 2,000+ houses, gold-wearing warrior-nobility, and trade connections to China, Japan, and India. Chinese tributary missions confirmed 1406-1411 (William Henry Scott, citing Ming records). Your Tamondong ancestors were part of this ruling class.
📄 William Henry Scott, "Barangay: Sixteenth-Century Philippine Culture and Society" | JSTOR: Erwin S. Fernandez (2010)
🧬 Abalos = Pre-Colonial Indigenous Name ✅ Primary Source
"Albalos" listed in Hector Santos' Bibingka Catalog of indigenous Filipino names that survived the 1849 Clavería Decree. The Abalos surname may predate Spanish colonial rule. Evolution: Albalos → Ávalos → Abalos. Also "Banaag" (Natividad's mother Maria Banaag) is indigenous.
📄 Bibingka Catalog (Wayback Machine) — Hector Santos
📍 Bacarro = Bacarra, Ilocos Norte ✅ 85%
Benita Bacarro's surname is a habitational name from Bacarra municipality, Ilocos Norte. Combined with the Daquigan surname from Tagudin, Ilocos Sur, the Daquigan-Bacarro marriage was an Ilocano endogamous union between migrant families in Pangasinan.
⛪ Three Family-Surname Priests in Bayambang Parish (1898-1928)
Fr. Francisco Fernandez (1898-1902), Fr. Agustin Rosario (1916-1920), Fr. Fermin Abalos (1920-1928) — three consecutive priests with your family surnames served at the bridge town between San Carlos and Gerona.
🏘️ Daquigan Political Dynasty in Gerona, Tarlac
Ronjie Daquigan — former Vice Mayor, current Councilor of Gerona (2022-present). Christian P. Daquigan — Acting Director, Bureau of Animal Industry (national government, 2025). The Daquigan family is a political force in the exact town where Bienvenido was born.
