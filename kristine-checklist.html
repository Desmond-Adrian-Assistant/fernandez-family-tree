⚔️ Lolo Bien's Father — Mariano Fernandez (1905–1945)

⚔️ Mariano Fernandez died on January 10, 1945 — the day after the Liberation of Luzon began

Full name: Mariano V(illanueva) Fernandez — the "V" confirms his mother was Marta Villanueva

Born: 1905, San Carlos City, Pangasinan

Died: January 10, 1945, Gerona, Tarlac, Philippines — age 40

Spouse: Victoriana Daquigan (b. 1917)

Source: Ancestry.com — Philippines Deaths & Burials (collection 60128, record 2032082), FHL Film 1969306

What happened in Gerona on that day:
Dec 1941USAFFE 21st Division (recruited from Tarlac & Pangasinan men) held Defensive Line D-3 at Gerona itself. These were Mariano's neighbors.
1942–1944Japanese occupation. Col. Edwin Ramsey's guerrillas (40,000 men) operated across Central Luzon including Gerona. Jack Laureano Soriano from San Carlos commanded a battalion. Mariano's principalía status makes guerrilla involvement probable.
Jan 9, 1945203,608 American troops land at Lingayen Gulf — 60 km north of Gerona. Japanese forces begin panicked retreat southward along MacArthur Highway, which runs directly through Gerona.
Jan 10, 1945Mariano Fernandez dies in Gerona. Day 2 of the invasion. Retreating Japanese forces committed widespread atrocities — explicit orders stated "All people on the battlefield will be put to death." Over 150,000 Filipino civilians died during the Battle of Luzon.
Jan 23, 1945American forces reach Clark Air Base (south of Gerona). Gerona was liberated within days of Mariano's death. He died on the razor's edge between occupation and freedom.

Bienvenido (Lolo Bien) was only 7 years old when his father died. He grew up fatherless, was raised by Victoriana alone, became an accountant, married Aurea in 1963, and brought the family to America by 1975. The Tamondong family also lost Pvt. Rafael C. Tamondong (US Army, died May 26, 1942 during the Fall of Bataan).
