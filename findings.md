## Surname Research

### Tamondong
- **Origin**: Pangasinan province
- **Bearers**: ~10,290 worldwide; 47% in Ilocos region, 18% NCR, 11% Central Luzon
- **1849 Catálogo**: Listed as a Pangasinan surname
- **Stronghold**: San Carlos City, Pangasinan (multiple notable bearers confirmed)
- **Etymology**: Borrowed from Pangasinan language into Tagalog

### Paglingayen
- **Origin**: Ilocano
- **Meaning**: From Ilocano "paglingay" = "to bend/curve"
- **Note**: Name echoes "Lingayen" — Lingayen Gulf is in Pangasinan
- **Bearers**: Very rare, found in ~10 countries

### Daquigan
- **Origin**: Ilocos/Central Luzon
- **Bearers**: ~1,781 worldwide; 27% Central Luzon, 20% Ilocos, 19% NCR
- **Very rare surname** — ranked 227,707th globally

### Bacarro
- **Origin**: Philippines
- **Bearers**: ~3,633 worldwide; 30% Northern Mindanao, 15% Central Visayas
- **Geographic outlier**: Unlike other family surnames, concentrated in Visayas/Mindanao rather than Ilocos/Pangasinan
- **Possible migration**: Benita Bacarro may have come from a different region

### Abalos
- **Origin**: Spanish Basque — from Ábalos, La Rioja, Spain
- **Meaning**: From Basque "abal" = "apple tree"
- **1849 Catálogo**: Listed
- **Prominent**: Benjamin Abalos Sr. (COMELEC chairman), Jason Abalos (actor)

### Rosario
- **Origin**: Spanish
- **Meaning**: "rosary" (from Latin "rosarium")
- **Bearers**: 38,383 in Philippines; **44% in Ilocos region**, 14% NCR
- **Very strong Ilocos connection**

### Villanueva
- **Origin**: Spanish/Pangasinan
- **Meaning**: "new town"
- **Listed as Pangasinan surname** on Wiktionary

### Fernandez
- **Origin**: Spanish patronymic ("son of Fernando")
- **Very common in Philippines**
- **Dagupan connection**: Congressman Angel B. Fernandez made San Carlos a city (1965)
- **Fercolla Clan**: Major Fernandez family documented in Dagupan

Detailed genealogy of the Fernandez-Cóquia-Llamas (FERCOLLA) clan:

## Dagupan Fernandez Political Dynasty (FERCOLLA Clan)
- Major city in Pangasinan province, Luzon
- The Fernandez family is one of the most powerful political dynasties in Dagupan
- **A.B. Fernandez Avenue** — a major avenue in Dagupan named after Angel B. Fernandez

### Dynasty Lineage (documented from Deo Antonio D. Llamas blog + court records)
1. **Don Antonio Llamas Fernandez** — Municipal President of Dagupan (1911-1915), married **Capistrana Bernal**
   - Children: **Aurelio** (businessman), **Jose** (publisher of Tunong Magazine 1920s-30s), **Amado** (doctor, built hospital, died WWII), **Paz** (married Eng. Isidoro de Venecia), **Miguel** (schoolteacher), **Angel** (mayor + congressman)
2. **Angel B. Fernandez** — mayor of Dagupan, later congressman, A.B. Fernandez Ave named after him
3. **Alipio Fernandez Sr** — son of Angel, mayor when Dagupan became chartered city (June 20, 1947)
4. **Alipio "Al" Fernandez Jr** — elected mayor 1992, served 3 terms, later Undersecretary of DILG, then Bureau of Immigration commissioner, died August 9, 2018 (age 75, lung cancer)
5. **Armando Cabal Fernandez** — grandson of Don Antonio, Vice Mayor of Dagupan, co-author of Fercolla Clan book, **Philippine consul general in Los Angeles**, founded **Pangasinan Brotherhood-USA (PB-USA)** in 1978
6. **Michael B. Fernandez** — current city councilor (since 2004), Knights of Columbus Grand Knight

### Fernandez-Coquia Connection
- **Don Modesto Hortaleza Coquia** (Municipal President 1907-1909) married **Genoveva Fernandez**
- This intermarriage created the FERCOLLA (Fernandez-Coquia-Llamas) clan
- The six Fernandez siblings (Jose, Amado, Miguel, Paz, Angel, Aurelio) co-owned fishpond property in Arellano-Bani, Dagupan (8,209 sqm, Lot 2991)
- The clan pioneered the milkfish (bangus) fishpond industry in Dagupan

### Current Dagupan Fernandez-Coquia Presence

## Philippine Supreme Court Case — MAJOR FIND (G.R. No. 143868)
- **Source**: https://elibrary.judiciary.gov.ph/thebookshelf/showdocs/1/51697
- **Case**: Oscar C. Fernandez, Gil C. Fernandez, and Armando C. Fernandez vs. Spouses Carlos and Narcisa Tarun (2002)
- **Subject**: Partition of fishpond property (Lot 2991, 8,209 sqm) in **Arellano-Bani, Dagupan City**
- **Key people in the case**:
  - **Angel Fernandez** married to **Corazon Cabal** — owned 7,114 sqm of the fishpond
  - Angel's siblings/co-owners: **Antonio, Santiago, Demetria, Armando** (all surnamed Fernandez)
  - Armando "died single and without issue"
  - Angel's children (the petitioners): **Oscar C. Fernandez, Gil C. Fernandez, Armando C. Fernandez**
  - Listed as "Corazon Cabal vda. de Fernandez" — meaning Angel predeceased her
- **Property**: Lot 2991, Cadastral Survey of Dagupan, originally OCT No. 43099, then TCT No. 24440
- **Note**: The "C." middle initial in Oscar, Gil, Armando = Cabal (from mother Corazon Cabal)
- **Connection**: "Armando Cabal Fernandez" is the same person who co-authored the FERCOLLA book AND was Vice Mayor of Dagupan!
- **This confirms the Fernandez family owned fishpond property in Arellano-Bani area of Dagupan**
