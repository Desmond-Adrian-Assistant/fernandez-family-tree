## Key Findings

### 🔥🔥 Dr. Sicat Names Bacarra, Badoc, and Sinait as Gerona's Ilocano Settler Towns

From the authoritative "History of Tarlac Towns" by Dr. Rodrigo M. Sicat:

> "In 1753, Pontalon was relocated and renamed Barug... Subsequently, **settlers from Ilocos were attracted to Barug, especially those from the towns of Bacarra, Badoc, and Sinait.**"

This is the **FOURTH** independent source confirming Bacarra, Ilocos Norte → Gerona migration:
1. **Dr. Rodrigo Sicat** (academic historian, wordpress page)
2. **Official Gerona Barangay Ayson history** (municipal website)
3. **Tarlakenyo.com** (local news, Jan 2023 republication)
4. **Bonarae.net Acob family** ("Descendants of Fernando Bernardo and Pastora Acob of Bacarra, Ilocos Norte and Gerona, Tarlac")

The Bacarro surname = Bacarra, Ilocos Norte origin is now **quadruple-corroborated** across completely independent sources.

### 🔥 Jorge Bocobo's Mother = Rita Teodora TABAGO y Cleofas

From Wikipedia:
- **Jorge Bocobo** — born October 19, 1886 in **Gerona, Tarlac**
- Associate Justice of the Supreme Court
- UP President (1934-1939), pensionado, translated Rizal's novels
- **Mother: Rita Teodora Tabago y Cleofas**

**TABAGO** is one of the Danzo barangay founding families:
- **Florentino Tabago** = 3rd teniente del barrio of Danzo
- **Arturo Tabago** ran for Mayor of Gerona in 2022

This means Jorge Bocobo — Gerona's most illustrious son — was from the SAME barangay network as the Fernandez tenientes. The Tabago, Fernandez, Benitez, Porlucas, Arciaga, and Cabacungan families of Danzo produced both barangay leaders AND Supreme Court justices.

|------|-------|
