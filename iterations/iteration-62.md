### 🔥 Key Discovery: 1st Mapandan Company (File 202)
An **independent guerrilla company** from Mapandan — the exact municipality where **Valeriano Tamondong** was one of 10 founding fathers (1887). This file (NARA RG 407, Entry 1087, File 202) would contain individual names of Mapandan guerrilla fighters. Given the Tamondong family's 224+ years of political leadership, Tamondong family members almost certainly served in this unit.

### LGAF Complete Squadron List (NARA Files 102-24 through 102-79)
The LGAF had numbered squadrons from 1 through 439+. Squadrons were geographic:
- **Sqdns. 200-229**: Pangasinan Military Area (15 squadrons under Capt. Ray C. Hunt)
- **Sqdns. 300-439**: Tarlac Military Area (6 squadrons under Capt. Al Hendrickson)
- **Sqdns. 401-402**: The specific "Flying Squadrons" covering Sta. Ignacia–Gerona

The LGAF plaque in Umingan (deciphered by Karl Welteke) confirms:
> "164 Guerrilla Squadrons and units with a total strength of an Army Division covering Pangasinan, Nueva Ecija, Tarlac and Pampanga"

By war's end (May 31, 1945), Lapham had 79 squadrons, 809 officers and 13,382 men, with 813 casualties.
