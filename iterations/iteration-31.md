## 🔥🔥 MAJOR CORRECTION: Fernandez Tenientes Are in Barangay DANZO, Not Don Basilio

Previous iterations stated the Fernandez tenientes were in "Barangay Don Basilio." This was INCORRECT. Re-reading the official Gerona municipal page reveals:

**Barangay Danzo** has the full teniente succession list that includes BOTH Fernandez:

> "The teniente Del barrio from the early days to the present is as follows: Mariano Benitez, Pablo Torato, Florentino Tabago, **Eulalia Fernandez**, Miguel Porlucas, Pedro Porlucas, Ignacio Urbano, Domingo Arciaga, Pampilo Arciaga, Pelgion Cabacungan, Ignacio Rombaoa, Sotero Maurera, Mariano Bugayong, Justo Balaba and **Exequiel Fernandez**."

### Danzo Key Facts
- **Name origin:** From "Ransouan, the **Pangasinan name** for a hiding or resting place" — confirms Pangasinan linguistic/cultural origins
- **First settlers:** "This group came from Pangasinan" — explicitly stated in the official history
- **Geography:** "Bounded on the **west by Barangay Tagumbao** and on the north by Barangay Don Basilio and on the east by Barangay Pinasling and on the south by Barangay Malayep"
- **Land area:** 262,725 sq m (262.7 hectares), 177 hectares agricultural
- **Crops:** Rice, sugarcane, corn, camote, sincamas, vegetables
- **Cultural practices:** "Bunyag ti siruk ti Latuk" (Ilocano baptism custom) — shows mixed Ilocano-Pangasinan community

### Why This Matters
- **Danzo borders Tagumbao directly** — the Fernandez and Capinding families were **literal neighbors**
- Mariano Fernandez (b. ~1905, San Carlos, Pangasinan) migrated to Gerona and lived in the Danzo-Tagumbao area
- His 2nd wife **Dionicia Capinding** (b. 1906) was from the **founding political family of Tagumbao** (adjacent barangay)
- Mariano's son Bienvenido was born in 1937, during the era when Fernandez and Capinding families both held barrio lieutenant positions
- The Danzo barrio was literally founded by **Pangasinan settlers** — same origin as the Fernandez family

### Fernandez Position in Danzo Leadership
Eulalia Fernandez was the **4th** teniente del barrio. Exequiel Fernandez was the **15th** (last listed). Given that the list spans from "early days" through modern era:
- Eulalia likely served during the American period (~1910s-1930s) — contemporary with Mariano Fernandez's arrival
- Exequiel likely served much later (~1960s-1980s?) — suggesting multi-generational Fernandez presence

## Geographic Analysis — Mariano's Migration Pattern

The Danzo barangay history confirms the Fernandez family's specific settlement pattern in Gerona:

1. **Pangasinan settlers** came to Gerona (which was Dominican territory under Bayambang)
2. They settled in what became **Barangay Danzo** — naming it with a Pangasinan word
3. Fernandez family rose to barrio leadership (Eulalia as 4th teniente)
4. Adjacent **Barangay Tagumbao** was the Capinding family's territory
5. Mariano Fernandez married Dionicia Capinding — a marriage between neighboring barrio elite families
6. Bienvenido Fernandez born March 22, 1937 — same year Patricio Capinding became Tagumbao barrio lieutenant

This parallels the San Carlos pattern exactly: principalía families from neighboring territories intermarrying to consolidate land and influence.
