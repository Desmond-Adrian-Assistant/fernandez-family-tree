### 2. Cervantes Municipality, Ilocos Sur — NEW Geographic Context

**Finding:** There is a real municipality named **Cervantes** in Ilocos Sur, Philippines (established 1879, named after Miguel de Cervantes the author). It is reached from the coast via the **Cervantes-Tagudin Road**.

**KEY GEOGRAPHIC LINK:**
- **Tagudin, Ilocos Sur** = the town where the **Daquigan surname** originates (1795 christening record of earlier Domingo Daquigan, confirmed Iteration 28)
- The **Cervantes municipality** is connected DIRECTLY to **Tagudin** via its only coastal road
- Both Cervantes (Ilocos Sur municipality) and Tagudin are in the southeastern Ilocos Sur mountain corridor

**Implication for Janice's Cervantes family:** The SURNAME "Cervantes" assigned to Ilocos Sur families under the 1849 Clavería Decree would apply to families IN the Cervantes municipality area. However, Kapampangans (Angeles City, Pampanga) were EXEMPT from the 1849 decree — their Cervantes surname predates 1849. The geographic Cervantes-Tagudin connection is most relevant as CONTEXT showing that:
1. Ilocano families who received the "Cervantes" surname in 1849 were the ones living near the Cervantes/Tagudin area
2. Janice's Angeles City Cervantes family has a DIFFERENT (pre-1849 Spanish/colonial) origin for their surname
3. The Daquigan-Tagudin connection and the Cervantes municipality-Tagudin road create an indirect Ilocos corridor linking both family sides geographically

**Assessment:** CONTEXTUAL finding — does not directly prove a relationship between Janice's Angeles City Cervantes and the Ilocano Cervantes municipality families, but adds interesting geographic texture.
