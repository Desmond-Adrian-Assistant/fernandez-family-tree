### Danzo Barangay History — Full Re-Read Confirmed
The official Gerona municipal website (geronatarlac.gov.ph/about-44-barangays/) confirms all previously documented Danzo details:
- Founded by Pangasinan settlers
- Name from "Ransouan" (Pangasinan for hiding/resting place)
- Ignacio Arciaga was the first settler
- Full teniente del barrio list: Mariano Benitez through Exequiel Fernandez (15 tenientes total)
- **Fernandez tenientes are #4 (Eulalia) and #15 (Exequiel)** — confirming the family was present from early days through the final listed period
- Geographic boundaries confirmed: west=Tagumbao, north=Don Basilio, east=Pinasling, south=Malayep

Additional cultural note: "The practice of exacting dowry is still observed by the parents of young ladies on the parents of prospective grooms" — traditional Filipino courtship practices maintained in Danzo.

### Gerona Town History — Key Details Confirmed
- 1704: Dominican missionaries from Bayambang begin converting "Paontalon" (original name)
- 1722: Recognized as village, part of Paniqui under Pangasinan province
- First settlers: "Ilocanos and Pangasinenses" — matches Fernandez-Daquigan family composition
- Pangasinenses named the settlement "Barug" (little forest) — outnumbered the Ilocanos
- 1765: Renamed Gerona after Gov-Gen Narciso Claveria's hometown (Girona, Catalonia)
- 1844: Became independent town
- 1845: First Gobernadorcillo — Don Anacleyo Melegrito
- 1873: Transferred to new Tarlac Province
