## Key Findings

### 🔥 Pangasinan Brotherhood-USA (PB-USA) — The Diaspora Principalía Network

PB-USA, founded by **Armando C. Fernandez** (FERCOLLA, consul general LA) in 1978, is the umbrella organization for all Pangasinan town/city associations in Southern California. It has **18 presidents** to date and is headquartered in the Long Beach/Cerritos area of LA.

**CRITICAL: Each Pangasinan town has its own association under PB-USA.** The **San Carlenian Organization** represents San Carlos City.

The PB-USA is **the exact same principalía network operating in the US diaspora** as the political families in Pangasinan. FERCOLLA Fernandez→Coquia→Cabal→Banaag→Soriano — all represented. The San Carlenian Organization is the specific channel for San Carlos City families. This could be a resource for connecting with families who knew Bienvenido/Aurea.

**KEY GAP: PB-USA is LA-based. There is NO equivalent dedicated Pangasinan organization in Chicago/Midwest found.** The Chicago Filipino community is organized differently — by profession (nurses), by general Filipino organizations (PACF, FACC), and by historical society (FAHSC). Bienvenido's family may not have had the same Pangasinan-specific community network in Chicago as exists in California.

## Assessment
This iteration reveals that the principalía network doesn't just operate in Pangasinan — it **extends to the US diaspora through PB-USA**. However, PB-USA is California-based. The Fernandez family emigrated to Chicago, not LA, which means they may not be connected to PB-USA. The lack of a dedicated Pangasinan organization in Chicago could make the family harder to trace through community networks, unless they were connected to the general Filipino community organizations (PACF, FACC, FAHSC). The FAHSC archives, currently being processed, represent the best untapped resource for Chicago-based Filipino family records.
