## Key Discovery: Gerona Was Historically PART OF Bayambang

### 🔥🔥🔥 The Complete Ecclesiastical Chain: San Carlos → Bayambang → Gerona

From the **official Bayambang municipal website** (bayambang.gov.ph):
> "In 1614, [Bayambang] became a visita of the vicariate of **Binalatongan [now San Carlos City]**."
> "Bayambang used to encompass the towns of Alcala and Santo Tomas, Pangasinan and those of **Paniqui, Gerona and Camiling, Tarlac**."

From the **official Gerona town history** (geronatarlac.gov.ph):
> "The Dominicans placed the Vicar of **Malanguey (now Bayambang, Pangasinan)** is charged with the mission of converting the local inhabitants of 'Paontalon' (original name of Gerona) to become Catholics."

**This means:**
1. **San Carlos (Binalatongan)** was the original Dominican vicariate (founded 1587)
2. **Bayambang** was a visita of San Carlos from 1614, became its own vicariate in 1619
3. **Gerona** was under Bayambang's jurisdiction — part of the same Dominican ecclesiastical territory
4. All three locations were connected through the **Dominican Order** for over 400 years

### Why Bienvenido Was Born in Gerona, Tarlac

This completely explains why Bienvenido (whose parents Mariano Fernandez + Victoria Daquigan were from the San Carlos/Pangasinan area) was **born in Gerona, Tarlac in 1937**:
- Gerona was part of the same Dominican-administered region as San Carlos and Bayambang
- The towns are geographically close (PhilAtlas confirms **San Carlos is one of the closest cities to Gerona**)
- Pangasinan families regularly settled in Gerona — the official town history states: "the first group of people who came and settled in Gerona are the **Ilocanos and Pangasinenses**"
- Mariano Fernandez (Bienvenido's father, b. ~1905) likely moved to Gerona for agricultural land — the town is rich farmland
- The road from San Carlos to Gerona passes through **Bayambang** — where THREE family-surname priests served (Fr. Francisco Fernandez 1898-1902, Fr. Agustin Rosario 1916-1920, Fr. Fermin Abalos 1920-1928)

## Gerona, Tarlac — Key Facts

- **Original name:** Paontalon
- **Founded:** 1844 as independent town (gobernadorcillo appointed 1845)
- **Province transfer:** Included in new Province of Tarlac when founded in 1873 (previously part of Pangasinan territory)
- **Population:** 97,937 (2024 census), 44 barangays
- **Languages:** Ilocano, Kapampangan, Tagalog (trilingual area at Pangasinan/Tarlac/Pampanga intersection)
- **Parish:** Saint Catherine of Alexandria Parish (founded 1894, Diocese of Tarlac)
- **Geography:** MacArthur Highway runs through center; agricultural land (rice, corn, sugarcane)
- **Settlers:** "Ilocanos and Pangasinenses" — matching the Fernandez-Daquigan family's Pangasinan/Ilocano roots

## Tarlac-Pangasinan Provincial Boundary Context

When **Tarlac province was created in 1873**, several towns that had been under Pangasinan jurisdiction were transferred to the new province — including Gerona, Paniqui, and Camiling. This means:
- Records before 1873 for Gerona would be in **Pangasinan provincial records**
- Church records for Gerona before 1894 (parish founding) would be under **Bayambang parish**
- The Fernandez/Daquigan family may have civil records in BOTH provinces
