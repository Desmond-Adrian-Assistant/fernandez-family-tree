## Analysis

### The Tamondong Principalía Dynasty — 224 Years of Leadership
The evidence now spans over two centuries:
- **1770s**: Marcos Cayabyab was Captain (a related/allied family)
- **1802**: Simon Tamondong was Captain, overseeing the church reconstruction
- **1850s**: Juan Tamondong (Adrian's ancestor) was alive — likely part of this ruling family
- **2007**: George Tamondong was a TV cameraman with close ties to the mayor
- **2020s**: Eduardo C. Tamondong is the current Barangay Captain of Sapinit

This is consistent with Philippine political dynasties — families that held principalía status during Spanish rule often maintained influence through the American period (1898-1946), independence, and into the present.

### The Macalanda-Tamondong Connection
The Ancestry record showed Pedro Caguioa Tamondong (b. 1903) had a mother named **Maria Macalanda**. Now we find **Ramil Macalanda** as the current barangay captain of Tandoc in San Carlos. This suggests the Macalanda family is another established San Carlos clan that intermarried with the Tamondongs.

### San Carlos Power Families Network
From the barangay officials list, we can see a network of recurring surnames that likely constitute the traditional ruling families:
- **Tamondong** (Sapinit) — Adrian's maternal ancestors
- **Rosario** (Balococ, Buenglat, Nelintap) — Adrian's maternal ancestors
- **Cayabyab** (Pangoloan, Turac) — 1770s-era captains, still in power
- **Macalanda** (Tandoc) — intermarried with Tamondong
- These families have maintained political positions for centuries
