## WWII Guerrilla Records — Path Forward Clarification

### Bamban WWII Museum — Key Resource
Two articles confirmed:
1. **Tarlac Military Area (TMA)** under Capt. Albert Hendrickson + local commander **Capt. Cris Hipolito** operated in **"the area of Sta. Ignacia – Gerona"**
2. **Flying Squadrons 401 and 402** covered this zone
3. Also operating: **Aglipay Battalion, Gen. Antonio Luna Regiment** under Col. Eusebio Aquino's **3rd Regional Command, HUKBALAHAP**
4. **Bruce Guerrillas** operated in South Tarlac Military District — ~5,000 Filipinos and Aeta Negritos
5. Bamban Museum author: **Rhonie Dela Cruz** — Consultant to Tarlac Provincial Tourism Office

The Bamban Museum articles confirm HUKBALAHAP (Hukbo ng Bayan Laban sa Hapon — People's Anti-Japanese Army) also operated in the Gerona area under the 3rd Regional Command. ## Gerona Barangay Histories — Patterns Confirmed

From the official Gerona municipal website (geronatarlac.gov.ph/about-44-barangays/):

### Migration Patterns Documented
Every barangay history confirms the same pattern:
- **Ilocano settlers** from Ilocos Norte/Sur formed the majority of Gerona's population
- Secondary groups: Pangasinenses, Pampangans, Tagalogs
- Settlement period: Late 19th century (1870s-1890s)
