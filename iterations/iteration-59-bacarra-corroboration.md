## 🔥 Gerona Complete Ethnic/Settlement Pattern (Dr. Sicat)

### Historical Timeline
- **Pre-1704:** Only Negritos (Aetas) in the area called "Paontalon"
- **1704:** Dominicans from Bayambang discover Paontalon
- **1718:** Listed as visita of Paniqui (then a Pangasinan town)
- **1753:** Relocated and renamed "Barug" (Pangasinan word for "little forest")
- **After 1753:** Ilocano settlers from **Bacarra, Badoc, and Sinait** attracted to Barug
- **1787:** Population reaches 461 families
- **1838:** Already a functioning town with 252 tribute payers and 1,260 inhabitants
- **1846:** Traditional founding date; permanent parish priest assigned
- **1851:** Renamed "Gerona" after Governor General Clavería's Spanish hometown
- **1877:** Father Ciano documents 4 language groups: Pangasinan, Ilocano (dominant), Tagalog, Pampango
- **1945:** Officially created as independent municipality; first gobernadorcillo: Don Anacleto Melegrito

### Ethnic Composition (1995 Census)
- **78.09% Ilocano** (dominant)
- **12.59% Tagalog**
- **8.83% Kapampangan**
- (Implied: ~0.5% Pangasinan and others)

### Significance for Fernandez Family
The Fernandez family in Danzo was part of the **Pangasinan minority** (from San Carlos) that settled among a predominantly Ilocano population. They intermarried with:
- **Daquigan family** (Ilocano origin — Ilocos Sur via Cagayan)
- **Bacarro family** (from Bacarra, Ilocos Norte)
- **Capinding family** (local Gerona elite, Tagumbao)
- **Abando family** (Ilocano origin, also in Gerona)

This was a **cross-ethnic principalía marriage network** — Pangasinan families (Fernandez, Villanueva) marrying Ilocano families (Daquigan, Bacarro, Abando) in a border zone town.
