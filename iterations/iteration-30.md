### Critical Timeline Overlap
- **Bienvenido Fernandez was born March 22, 1937 in Gerona, Tarlac**
- **Patricio Capinding became barrio lieutenant of Tagumbao in 1937** — the exact same year!
- Mariano Fernandez's 2nd wife **Dionicia Capinding (b. 1906)** was almost certainly from this Tagumbao Capinding family
- The Capinding family was the political elite of Tagumbao — Mariano married into a prominent Gerona family

### Tagumbao Barangay History — Pangasinan Settlers First!
The official barangay history states the first settlers were **"a Group of Pangasinan hunters"** who named the place from their dialect:
> "It happened that a Group of Pangasinan hunter reached this place. These brought with them some roosters to be used as decoys to lure wild fowls."
> "TAWWA-TAWWA plants which the Pangasinan hunters called 'TAGUMBAO' in their dialect"

This was followed by Ilocano settlers from Bacarra (Ilocos Norte) and Sinait (Ilocos Sur) — the exact same Pangasinan → Ilocano settlement pattern as the Fernandez-Daquigan family.

### Tagumbao WWII — Capinding as Protector
Patricio Capinding's achievements during WWII are striking:
- **Prevention of maltreatment of civilians by Japanese soldiers** through tactful intervention
- **Acting as mediator to avoid clashes between Japanese soldiers and guerrillas**
- Finding markets for foodstuffs with the Japanese

This mirrors the guerrilla activity documented in Pangasinan (LGAF, Army of the Agno, ECLGA). Gerona was in the same occupation zone. Mariano Fernandez (living in the same area) would have known Patricio Capinding personally — his wife Dionicia was from this family.

### DAR Land Case: Capinding vs Cayabyab (1993)
From the Department of Agrarian Reform (O.P. Case No. 3875):
- **Antonio Capinding** owned CLT No. 303964 covering 3.1360 hectares of riceland in **Lawy, Capas, Tarlac**

- Capinding was deceived into signing an Affidavit of Voluntary Surrender
- Case resolved in Capinding's favor — land ownership restored

## Gerona Barangay Don Basilio — The Fernandez Barangay

From the official Gerona municipal website:
- **Barangay Don Basilio** was formerly a sitio of **Barangay Buenlag** (became independent barangay 1956)
- Original name: "Caniugan"
- **All tenientes del barrio listed**: Mariano Benitez, Pablo Torato, Florentino Tabago, **Eulalia Fernandez**, Miguel Porlucas, Pedro Porlucas, Ignacio Urbano, Domingo Arciaga, Pampilo Arciaga, Pelgion Cabacungan, Ignacio Rombaoa, Sotero Maurera, Mariano Bugayong, Justo Balaba and **Exequiel Fernandez**

### Don Basilio → Tagumbao Geographic Relationship
Both barangays are in western/central Gerona:
- **Don Basilio** = formerly part of Buenlag (central Gerona)
- **Tagumbao** = ~4 km west of Poblacion
- Close proximity — the Fernandez (Don Basilio/Buenlag area) and Capinding (Tagumbao) families were neighbors
