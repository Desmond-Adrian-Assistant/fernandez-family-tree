## Key Findings

### Silew Magazine — Timeline Now Precisely Dated

New data from yodisphere.com 2022 article and svillafania.wordpress.com 2011 article:

- **Silew** (meaning "light") — monthly magazine published **1934–1943** (previously unclear; now confirmed)
- **Maria Prado Magsano** (1893–1968) was editor of Silew **1936–1948** and founder of the Pangasinan Courier
- **Sandiy Silew** ("Silew Section") = the vernacular section of the Pangasinan Courier
- Publication chronology: Lioaoa (1915–1932) → Tonung (1928–1935) → **Silew (1934–1943)** → Sandiy Silew in Pangasinan Courier (post-WWII) → Sunday Punch (1954+)
- After Magsano, Pangasinan literary output declined sharply ("Literary Silence 1960s–1990s")
- Francisco Rosario is listed consistently as one of the 5 notable Silew/Sandiy Silew fiction writers alongside: Juan Villamil, Leonarda Carrera, Nena Mata, Maria Magsano

### Critical Age Assessment for Adrian's Francisco Rosario

Adrian's great-great-grandfather **Francisco Rosario** married **Juana Gutierrez** and fathered **Filomena Rosario** (who married Hilario Abalos and became Aurea's mother). Timeline:
- Filomena born ~1900–1915 (must be born before Aurea's 1936–1940 birth)
- Francisco Rosario therefore born ~1875–1900
- **Silew ran 1934–1943**: Francisco (born ~1875–1900) would have been **35–68 years old** during the Silew era — squarely within the age range for a working writer
