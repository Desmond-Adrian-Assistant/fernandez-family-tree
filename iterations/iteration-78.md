## Key Findings

### 1. NARA Guerrilla Unit Recognition Files — Complete Mapping for Gerona/San Carlos Area

From the NARA Alphabetical List of Guerrilla Units (archives.gov), THREE overlapping guerrilla organizations operated in the Gerona-Pangasinan border area where Mariano Fernandez lived. Here are the specific NARA file codes:

**Confirmed (Bamban WWII Museum):**
- Commander: **Captain Albert A. Hendrickson** (429th Signal Maintenance Company, US Army)

### 2. NEW: Hendrickson Promoted to Major (Not Just Captain)
Multiple Bamban Museum articles confirm Hendrickson was initially Captain, later promoted to **Major** Albert A. Hendrickson by 1945. His unit affiliation: **429th Signal Maintenance Company, US Army**.

### 3. Mia Calma — Woman Guerrilla Fighter from TMA, LGAF
A previously undocumented woman guerrilla fighter named **Mia Calma** served in the **Tarlac Military Area, LGAF** — the same unit covering Gerona. If Mariano Fernandez served in the guerrillas, he would have been in the same command structure.

### 4. HUKBALAHAP Presence in Gerona (Post-War)
From the Gerona barangay histories (Barangay Carbonel):
- During WWII, a guerrilla headquarters was established in Carbonel (neighboring barangay to Danzo)
- After WWII, the HUKBALAHAP occupied nearby barangays including Carbonel
- Philippine Constabulary raided Carbonel multiple times (1948+)
- Residents were forced to evacuate
- **The Cojuangco Hacienda** was present in Gerona — overseer Dominador Punzalan was assassinated

This confirms Gerona was a contested zone between USAFFE guerrillas (TMA/LGAF) and HUKBALAHAP — Mariano Fernandez would have experienced both.

### 5. 1st Mapandan Company (Independent) — File 202
The **1st Mapandan Co. (Ind)** was a recognized WWII guerrilla unit with its own NARA file (202). Mapandan is the town founded by **Valeriano Tamondong** in 1887. This independent company may have included Tamondong family members. The file could contain names of guerrilla fighters from a Tamondong-founded municipality.

### 6. CALAYAAN Barangay History — USAFFE 23rd Regiment Retreated Through Gerona
The official history of Barangay Calayaan (western Gerona) documents:
> "The retreat of the USAFFE particularly the 23rd Regiment, 21st Division under the command of Col. Liberato Littawa took place from the Poblacion to this barrio."

Additionally, **Pedro Tabago** (one of the Danzo founding family tenientes) disappeared during the war — listed among the four wartime disappearances from Calayaan. This confirms Danzo-area families were directly affected by WWII combat operations.

## NARA Records — Still NOT Digitized Online
The guerrilla unit recognition files remain **physical-only** at NARA College Park, MD (Record Group 407, Entry 1087, 1,665 boxes). The PVAO digitization project (funded 2015) has scanned some files but they are NOT publicly searchable by individual name online. The PVAO collections website (collections.pvao.mil.ph) was inaccessible during this research (Cloudflare/fetch failures).
