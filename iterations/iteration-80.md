## Key Findings

### Cervantes Surname — Global Statistics (Forebears.io, 2014)
- **479,445 bearers worldwide** — 1,120th most common globally
- **Most prevalent:** Mexico (304,004 = 63% of all Cervantes)
- **Philippines: 33,562** bearers (rank 217th nationally, 1 in 3,016 people)
- **United States: 62,355** (13% of total)
- **Origin:** "One who came from Cervantes, in Spain; descendant of Cervanto."
- Spanish name from Toledo. Descended from Knight Nuño Alonso born in Galicia (alcalde of Toledo, 12th century).
- **User reference:** Studies in Spain say Cervantes is of Jewish (Sephardic) origin, derived from "Servent" — changed to Cervantes to avoid Christian persecution. Similar to Abalos (possible Sephardic origin).
- **NOT in 1849 Catálogo de Apellidos** — predates the decree, suggesting possible actual Spanish ancestry or very early colonial adoption
- Philippines variants documented: Cervantis (18 bearers, highest density Philippines), Cirvantes (4, Philippines), Gervantes (12, highest Philippines)

### Cabrera Surname — Global Statistics (Forebears.io, 2014)
- **1,116,506 bearers worldwide** — 469th most common globally
- **Most prevalent:** Mexico (226,645), Argentina, Cuba, Spain, United States
- **Philippines: 69,376** bearers (rank 87th nationally, 1 in 1,459 people — VERY COMMON)
- **Highest density:** Northern Mariana Islands (1 in 49!) — Guam-adjacent Pacific Filipino community
- **Origin:** Spanish — "One who takes care of goats, a goatherd" (from Latin "caprarius")
- Also: Catalan habitational name from places called Cabrera
- Philippines: Widespread across all regions — not regionally concentrated
- Philippines variants: Cabrira (74), Cabreros (6,909 — separate surname)
- **Cabrera in Mangaldan, Pangasinan:** Fernando Juan Cabrera serves as Vice Mayor of Mangaldan (documented iteration 14) — same Cabrera surname as Natividad Cabrera Cervantes (Janice's mother)
