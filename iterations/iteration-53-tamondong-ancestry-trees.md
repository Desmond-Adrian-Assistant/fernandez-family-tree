## 🔥 Charlemagne T. Tamondong Sr. — Founding President of PAMET

### Biography
- **Full name:** Charlemagne T. Tamondong Sr.
- **Parents:** **Segundo Cardel** (father) + **Leonora Tamundong** (mother)
- **Born:** Philippines (birth date unknown)
- **Died:** March 26, 2012, Metro Manila
- **Burial:** Loyola Memorial Park, Parañaque, Metro Manila
- **Funeral home:** Funeraria Paz, Parañaque

### Career — Founding President of PAMET (1963-1967)
- **PAMET** = Philippine Association of Medical Technologists, Inc.
- Founded September 15, 1963 by Crisanto G. Almario at the Public Health Laboratory, Sta. Cruz, Manila
- First convention and election: September 20, 1964 at Far Eastern University
- **Charlemagne T. Tamondong became the first President** (served 1963-1967)
- Key achievements as president:
  - Lobbied for **House Bill No. 7682** for Medical Technologists — with help of Congressman Jose Moreno and Congresswoman Magnolia Antonio
  - This eventually became **RA 5527** (Philippine Medical Technology Act, enacted June 21, 1969)
  - Worked for PAMET's affiliation with IAMLT (International Association of Medical Laboratory Technologists) in Europe on May 28, 1970
  - Built PAMET's public recognition and institutional standing
  - Worked for amendments to PAMET Constitution and By-Laws (approved at 1969 National Convention at UST)
- PAMET is now the ONLY accredited professional organization for all registered Medical Technologists in the Philippines
- 46 provincial chapters + 1 international chapter (Singapore), 13,000+ members
- **Source:** Official PAMET website (pametinc.ph/about/), IFCC history document, multiple Philippine MedTech university course materials

### 🔥 Naming Convention Revelation
- Charlemagne's birth surname was **CARDEL** (father: Segundo Cardel)
- He used **TAMONDONG** as his professional/public surname — from his mother **Leonora Tamundong**
- The "T." middle initial = Tamondong (mother's maiden name, following Filipino naming convention)
- This means he chose to be known by his MATERNAL surname — possibly indicating the Tamondong name carried more prestige/connection than Cardel
- Variant spelling: "Tamundong" (from Ancestry record) vs "Tamondong" (standard) — common variation
