## Key Findings

### 🔥 MAJOR: Pangasinan WWII Guerrilla Units Identified

**Three major guerrilla organizations operated in Pangasinan:**

1. **Luzon Guerrilla Army Forces (LGAF)** — Led by Major Robert Lapham
   - **15 squadrons in Pangasinan** under **Captain Ray C. Hunt Jr.**
   - 38 squadrons in Nueva Ecija under Captain Harry McKenzie
   - Total ~13,000 Filipino fighters across Pangasinan, Nueva Ecija, Tarlac, Nueva Vizcaya
   - Based in Lupao/Umingan, Pangasinan (eastern part of province)
   - **NARA file: "Barker Unit LGAF"** and multiple regimental files (102-xx series)
   - Lapham was based in Chicago before the war (Burroughs Corporation)!

2. **Army of the Agno / 1st Organized Pangasinan Army Corps** — Filipino-organized
   - Named after the **Agno River** which flows through San Carlos City
   - Had Eastern, Western, and Central Divisions plus Central Brigade
   - **NARA files: 185-1 through 185-30** — extensive records
   - Units included:
     - 1st Regt, Eastern Division (185-5)
     - 1st Regt, Western Division (185-7)
     - 1st Regt, Central Brigade (185-10)
     - 4th Regt, Central Division (185-13)
     - 5th Regt, Central Division (185-14)
     - 5th Inf & 25th Replacement Regt (185-30)
     - 1st Nueva Ecija Regt, Central Division (185-28)

3. **1st Pangasinan Regiment, PTMD, ECLGA** (file 308-23) — Part of the East Central Luzon Guerrilla Area

**Significance for Mariano Fernandez (b. ~1905):**
- He was 36-40 years old during the Japanese occupation (1941-45)
- San Carlos City was within LGAF operating area (Pangasinan)
- The "Army of the Agno" was named after the river running through his home area
- The 21st Infantry Division (Philippine Army) drew troops from Pangasinan before the war
- As a principalía-connected family (through Tamondong), he may have held a leadership role

### 🔥 DISCOVERY: Valeriano Tamondong — Founding Father of Mapandan (1887)

- **Valeriano Tamondong** was one of **10 founding leaders** who conceived of separating Mapandan from Mangaldan as a new municipality
- Mapandan established **December 28, 1887** by Governor General Emilio Bravo
- Mapandan is **21 km from San Carlos City** and 16 km from Dagupan
- Borders: Mangaldan (NE), San Jacinto (NW), Manaoag (SE), Santa Barbara (SW)
- **Timeline**: Valeriano Tamondong (1887 Mapandan founder) → Simon Tamondong (1802 Captain of San Carlos) → the Tamondong line extends over nearly a century of confirmed political leadership, from at least 1802 to 1887+

### Robert Lapham — Chicago Connection!

- Lapham worked for Burroughs Corporation in **Chicago** before the war
- He was awarded the Distinguished Service Cross by MacArthur
- His guerrilla army covered Pangasinan extensively
- The Filipino veterans who served under LGAF may have maintained connections with Lapham's Chicago network
- **Possible immigration pathway**: Filipino LGAF veterans → connection to Lapham → Chicago
- This could explain why Bienvenido Fernandez ended up in Chicago specifically
