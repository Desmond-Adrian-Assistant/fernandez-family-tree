## Key Findings

### 🔥 MAJOR: Col. Edwin Ramsey's ECLGA Operated Directly in San Carlos

From a 2017 Positively Filipino article by Sandie Gillis (whose family hosted Ramsey in San Carlos):

- **Col. Edwin Ramsey** (U.S. Army, 26th Cavalry Regiment) built the **East Central Luzon Guerrilla Area (ECLGA)** resistance network
- In **1943**, while organizing guerrillas in **Bayambang, Pangasinan** (adjacent to San Carlos), Ramsey heard of **Judge Juan Benitez** from San Carlos
- Ramsey was **hidden in the Benitez family home in San Carlos** — the nipa hut behind their house became the guerrilla meeting/induction point
- **Captain Charles Putnam** (American) was appointed commanding officer of the **Pangasinan-Tarlac Military District (PTMD)**, a division of ECLGA
- Juan Benitez became deputy CO, then full CO after the Japanese captured and beheaded Capt. Putnam
- By war's end, Ramsey was leading **~40,000 Filipino guerrillas**
- In **1964**, the Benitez/Gillis family hosted a **homecoming reunion for Ramsey in San Carlos** — "ECLGA Veterans Reunion"
- Ramsey lobbied U.S. Congress 3 times for Filipino veterans' recognition (Congressional Gold Medal, 2016)

**Significance for Mariano Fernandez (b. ~1905):**
- San Carlos was a **direct recruitment center** for ECLGA guerrillas
- Guerrilla inductions happened in the town itself, not in remote mountains
- As a ~38-year-old man from a principalía family (Tamondong connection), Mariano would have been a prime recruit
- The PTMD records (NARA file 308-23) would list individual guerrilla members from San Carlos

### Tamondong in 1849 Catálogo — Also Assigned in Tarlac Province

From the Byahero blog on Tarlac City history:
- The 1849 Clavería Decree assigned surnames starting with "T" to Tarlac
- **"Tamondong"** is explicitly listed among Tarlac surnames (alongside Taala, Taar, Tabamo, Taban, etc.)
- This means Tamondong was in the Catálogo and assigned to **both Pangasinan and Tarlac** provinces
- The surname may have originated in Pangasinan and been distributed to neighboring provinces
