### Francisco Rosario — Pangasinan Literary Figure

A **Francisco Rosario** was a notable Pangasinan-language fiction writer in the early-to-mid 20th century:
- Published short stories in **Silew** ("Light") and **Sandiy Silew** magazines — the main Pangasinan vernacular literary publications
- Contemporaries: Juan Villamil, Leonarda Carrera, Nena Mata, Maria Magsano
- Maria Magsano (born early 1900s) passed her civil service exam and taught at Philippine Normal College for 20 years — suggesting these were middle-class educated writers
- The Silew magazine was active in the 1930s-60s; Sandiy Silew appears to be a successor/variant
- A "Francisco Rosario" (great-great-grandfather of Adrian, married Juana Gutierrez) would have been born ~1875-1895 — making him the RIGHT AGE to be a literary writer in the 1920s-1940s

**Assessment:** The Francisco Rosario who wrote in Silew magazine COULD be the same Francisco Rosario who is Adrian's great-great-grandfather. If so, this would be an extraordinary cultural find — a family member who was a recognized Pangasinan literary figure. However, without biographical details of the writer (birthplace, hometown, parents' names), this cannot be confirmed. Francisco Rosario is not a rare name.

**Research path:** The Philippine National Library (Manila) or the Pangasinan provincial library may hold archives of Silew magazine with biographical notes on contributors.
