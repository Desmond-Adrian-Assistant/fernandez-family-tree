## Key Findings

### Kapampangan Surname System — Comprehensive Summary (siuala.com)
The siuala.com article confirms multiple distinct Kapampangan surname classes:
- **PAMAGAT** — titles of nobility: Gatbonton, Gatmaitan, Gatdúlâ
- **PANDAMLÂ** — Sanskrit-origin surnames: Galúrâ (गरुड), Lacsínâ (दक्षिण), Lacsamána (लक्ष्मण) — from 10th century Buddhist era
- **PANDIUÁTÂ** — surnames for pure-blood descendants of DIUÁTÂ 'gods': Lakandanum, Lakanlále
- **PANGGAMPÂ** — hereditary occupational clan surnames: Mánansálâ, Mánalastas, Mánalóto

**Key legal confirmation:** "In 1849, Kapampángans were exempted from Claveria's decree that attempted to standardise the surnames... in 1649, the Spanish king granted Kapampángans autonomy and exempted them from tribute for all eternity." — siuala.com (citing original Spanish Royal Decree of May 20, 1649)

**Implications for Cervantes and Cabrera:**
- Both are **Spanish-form surnames** in Kapampangan territory
- Both predate 1849 decree (confirmed previously via Forebears.io — NOT in Catálogo)
- Since Kapampángans were exempt, these families retained their surnames voluntarily
- Spanish surnames in this context = either actual Spanish/mestizo descent, OR principalía families who adopted Spanish names as status markers during early colonial period (pre-1649)
- The Lacsamana family (Bona Rae Villarta's line) is also Pampanga-origin — and uses a Sanskrit-based surname — showing the diversity of Kapampangan naming traditions
