## Key Findings

### Cervantes Surname Research
- **Origin:** Spanish, from Latin "cervus" (stag). Named after Cervantes, Spain. Same etymology as Miguel de Cervantes (Don Quixote author)
- **Philippines incidence:** 33,562 bearers (ranked 217th most common surname)
- **NOT in the 1849 Catálogo de Apellidos** — like Fernandez, this is a pre-existing Spanish surname, suggesting possible Spanish ancestry or early colonial adoption
- **Pampanga connection confirmed:** Multiple Cervantes figures are active in Pampanga:
  - **Diosdado Cervantes** — owner of "DL Cervantes Construction" based in San Simon, Pampanga (near Angeles City). Quoted in 2018 Philstar article about flood control project collapse. **This could be Janice's father or a relative** — same name, same province!
  - **"Ding" Cervantes** — prominent Pampanga-based journalist, correspondent for Philippine Star, SunStar Pampanga, and Banat News. Based in Central Luzon (San Fernando/Angeles area). Received death threats in 2012 while covering news

### Cabrera Surname Research  
- **Origin:** Spanish/Catalan, from Latin "caprarius" (goatherd). Habitation name from places called Cabrera
- **Philippines incidence:** 69,376 bearers (ranked 87th — very common)
- **NOT in the 1849 Catálogo** — another pre-existing Spanish surname
- **Both Cervantes AND Cabrera** are pre-decree Spanish surnames, suggesting Natividad Cabrera Cervantes's family may have had actual Spanish ancestry or very early colonial naming

### Angeles City Context (Janice's Hometown)
- **Founded:** 1796 by Don Angel Pantaleon de Miranda, became separate municipality Dec 8, 1829
- **Chartered as city:** 1963
- **Key parish:** Holy Rosary Parish Church (Santo Rosario Church / Pisamban Maragul) — founded October 18, 1877
- **Archdiocese:** Archdiocese of San Fernando (est. 1949)
- **Clark Air Base:** US military installation 1903-1991, 3 miles west of Angeles City
  - Major employer for Angeles City residents
  - Many Filipino families with Clark Air Base employment connections emigrated to the US
  - Base closed November 21, 1991 (after Mt. Pinatubo eruption June 15, 1991)
- **Prominent families:** Nepomuceno, Henson (founding families), not Cervantes
