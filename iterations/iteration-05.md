## Key Findings

### 1. 1849 Catálogo de Apellidos — Surname Assignment System
- Governor-General Narciso Clavería y Zaldúa issued the decree on November 21, 1849
- The catalogue of 61,000 surnames was distributed to provincial heads, then to each barangay's parish priest
- **Surnames starting with "A" went to provincial capitals; "B" to secondary towns; "C" to tertiary towns**
- Each family chose or was assigned a surname; one surname per family per municipality
- **"Fernandez" is NOT in the Catálogo** — it's a Spanish patronymic already in use before 1849
- Families with pre-existing surnames (like Fernandez) were exempt from the decree
- **Implication for the family**: The Fernandez surname likely predates 1849 in Dagupan, suggesting possible Spanish ancestry or early colonial adoption. The "native" surnames in the family (Tamondong, Daquigan, Paglingayen, Bacarro) were likely assigned from the Catálogo.

### 2. Dagupan City Historical Timeline
- **1580**: Part of Alcadia Mayor de Pangasinan
- **1583**: Originally called "Bacnotan" by Augustinian missionaries
- **1660**: Malong Revolt; renamed "Nandaragupan" ("where people meet")
- **1780**: Simplified to "Dagupan"
- **1855**: Sual opened as foreign trade port
- **1898**: Liberated from Spanish rule by Katipuneros led by Juan Quesada and Eliseo Arzadon of Dagupan
- **WWII**: Dagupan served as Japanese provincial capital
- **January 9, 1945**: Lingayen Gulf Landing — massive Allied amphibious assault, beaches at Dagupan
- **Gen. MacArthur** set up his Luzon HQ in Dagupan after the landing
- **June 20, 1947**: Dagupan became a chartered city (RA 170); **Alipio Fernandez Sr. was first mayor**
- **July 16, 1990**: 7.7 earthquake caused severe damage (ground liquefaction)

### 3. WWII Records Resources
- **FilVetREP National Veterans Registry** — database of Filipino WWII veterans (Congressional Gold Medal awardees). Not searchable online; applications via regional directors.
- **PVAO Philippine Archives Collection** — 1,665 boxes at NARA College Park, MD. Guerrilla unit recognition files searchable at collections.pvao.mil.ph (site was down during search). Can search by last name, unit name, or location (Pangasinan).
- **NARA Philippine Army Records** — SF-180 form requests to National Personnel Records Center, St. Louis, MO
- **Bienvenido was born 1933** — he would have been 8-12 during WWII. Too young to serve, but his father Mariano (~1905) could have been a guerrilla fighter (age ~36-40 during occupation).
- **Key resource to try later**: PVAO guerrilla records search for "Fernandez" in Pangasinan

### 6. Bonuan Gueset — Abalos & de Venecia Connection
- Jose de Venecia Jr. (d. Feb 10, 2026) funeral held at **Annunciation of the Lord Parish in Bonuan Gueset, Dagupan**
- The Abalos family was previously documented in **Bonuan Gueset barangay**
- The Geni.com Dagupan project lists **"de Venecia y Ábalos"** as one of the family surnames
- The FERCOLLA clan is connected to de Venecia through **Paz Fernandez** who married **Eng. Isidoro de Venecia**
- **Network map**: Fernandez ↔ de Venecia (marriage of Paz) ↔ Ábalos (Geni "de Venecia y Ábalos" surname)
- This suggests the Abalos family married into the de Venecia family at some point, creating another link between the Fernandez and Abalos clans in Dagupan
