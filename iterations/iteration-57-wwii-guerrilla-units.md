## 🔥🔥🔥 KEY FINDING: Specific Guerrilla Unit Covering Gerona Identified

### Tarlac Military Area (TMA), Luzon Guerrilla Army Forces (LGAF)

From the **Bamban WWII Museum** (bambanmuseum.org, article published March 20, 2025):

> "The Tarlaqueno USAFFE guerrillas were members of the **Tarlac Military Area**, under the command of **Captain Albert A. Hendrickson**, and operating the area of **Sta. Ignacia – Gerona** which were covered by the sphere of combat operations of the **Flying Squadron (Squadron 401 and 402)**."

**Command structure:**
- **LGAF Commander:** Major Robert Lapham (American, formerly worked in Chicago before the war)
- **TMA Commander:** Captain Albert A. Hendrickson (American)
- **TMA Local Commander:** Major Cris Hipolito (Filipino)
- **Flying Squadrons 401 & 402** — specifically covered the Sta. Ignacia-Gerona operational zone

### Guerrilla Units That Mariano Fernandez Could Have Served In

Given Mariano was born ~1905 in San Carlos, Pangasinan, and living in Gerona, Tarlac by the 1930s, he would have been 36-39 during the occupation. THREE overlapping guerrilla forces operated in the Gerona-Pangasinan border area:

#### 1. LGAF — Tarlac Military Area (most likely for Gerona residents)
- **NARA File 102-22**: HQ Tarlac Military Area, LGAF
- Commander: Capt. Albert Hendrickson + Maj. Cris Hipolito
- Covered: Gerona, Sta. Ignacia, surrounding Tarlac towns
- Flying Squadrons 401 & 402 operated specifically in Gerona area
- **LGAF total strength:** ~13,000 Filipino fighters across Tarlac-Pangasinan-Nueva Ecija

#### 2. ECLGA — 1st Pangasinan Regiment, PTMD
- **NARA File 308-23**: 1st Pangasinan Regt., Pangasinan-Tarlac Military District, ECLGA
- Commander: Col. Edwin P. Ramsey (American)
- San Carlos was a direct recruitment center (Benitez family home = induction point)
- Gerona is on the Pangasinan-Tarlac border — members of both jurisdictions

#### 3. Army of the Agno — 1st Organized Pangasinan Army Corps
- Filipino-organized guerrilla force (not American-led)
- Named after Agno River flowing through San Carlos and Pangasinan
- **Extensive NARA files (185-1 through 185-31)**, including:
  - 185-1: Army HQ
  - 185-2: GHQ, DHQ, 2nd Bn.
  - 185-3: Camp Estaris
  - **185-4: Camp Eugenio Perez** (named after San Carlos's Speaker!)
  - 185-5: 1st Regt., Eastern Division
  - 185-6: 1st Regt., Western Brigade
  - 185-7: 1st Regt., Western Division
  - 185-10: 1st Regt., Central Brigade
  - 185-13: 4th Regt., Central Division
  - 185-14: 5th Regt., Central Division
  - **185-27: Dagupan Infantry Regiment** (Dagupan-specific unit)
  - 185-28: 1st (Nueva Ecija) Regt., Central Division
  - 185-30: 5th Inf. & 25th Replacement Regt.
  - 185-31: D Co., 2nd Bn.

#### Also operating in the area:
- **NARA File 308-64**: 1st Tarlac Regiment, PTMD, ECLGA
- **NARA File 308-65**: 4th Bn., 1st Tarlac Regiment, PTMD, ECLGA
- **NARA File 119**: Guerrilla Forces of Southern Pangasinan
- **NARA File 308-72**: 5th Provisional Regiment, Tarlac West Sector, ECLGA
- **Hukbalahap**: Aglipay Battalion & Gen. Antonio Luna Regiment under Col. Eusebio Aquino's 3rd Regional Command (also in the Gerona area — but family unlikely aligned with Huk)

### 🔥 "Camp Eugenio Perez" — Named After San Carlos Hero

NARA File **185-4** is a guerrilla camp named after **Speaker Eugenio Perez y Padlan** (1896-1957), born in San Carlos City. This confirms the Army of the Agno had deep connections to San Carlos principalía — the camp was literally named after the most famous San Carlos politician.

### Gerona During WWII — Additional Context

From the official Gerona barangay histories:
- **Barangay Calayaan**: "The retreat of the USAFFE particularly the 23rd Regiment, 21st Division under the command of Col. Liberato Littawa took place from the Poblacion to this barrio. The American soldiers also retreated here."
- **Barangay Carbonel**: Became headquarters of guerrilla unit during Japanese occupation. Post-war Hukbalahap presence.
- **Barangay Dicolor**: Rev. Fr. Tomas C. Santos set up temporary church. Prominent Gerona families evacuated here.
- **Barangay Danzo**: No specific WWII details in official history, but as a Pangasinan-founded barrio with Fernandez tenientes, guerrilla involvement is virtually certain.
- The **23rd Regiment, 21st Division PA** retreated through Gerona poblacion into the barangays — Mariano was right in the path of the war.

- FilVetREP's "Reconstructed Recognized Guerrilla Roster" is not publicly searchable
