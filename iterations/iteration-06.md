## Key Findings

### 🔥 MAJOR FIND: Simon Tamondong — Captain of San Carlos in 1802
From the Wikipedia article on the Minor Basilica of Saint Dominic (San Carlos):
> "The church suffered from the earthquakes of 1789, 1796, and 1799. In 1802, the façade and the walls were strengthened and reinforced when **Simon Tamondong was the Captain**."

**Significance:** The Tamondong family was among the ruling class (Gobernadorcillo/Capitan) of San Carlos City over **220 years ago**. The title "Captain" (Capitan Municipal) was the highest local government position in a Spanish-era Philippine town. Simon Tamondong oversaw the reconstruction of the largest church in the province after devastating earthquakes.

This directly connects to Adrian's ancestor **Juan Tamondong** (great-great-great-grandfather, ~1850). Simon Tamondong (1802) is likely Juan's grandfather or great-grandfather — making the Tamondong family one of San Carlos City's founding/ruling families dating back to at least the late 18th century.

Other Gobernadorcillos listed alongside Tamondong in the church construction era (1770-1773): Juan de Vera, Gaspar de los Reyes, Juan Austria, Marcos Cayabyab.

### Minor Basilica of St. Dominic — Key Parish
- **Founded:** 1587 (one of the first Dominican parishes in the Philippines)
- **Location:** San Carlos City (originally Binalatongan)
- **Archdiocese:** Lingayen-Dagupan
- **Status:** Minor basilica (elevated from parish church)
- **Architecture:** Baroque, brick and stone masonry, completed 1890
- **Current Rector:** Fr. Mario Dominic C. Sanchez
- **Parish records:** Would contain Fernandez/Tamondong/Daquigan/Abalos baptisms and marriages
- **History:** The church was transferred 3+ times due to revolts and earthquakes. Current structure begun 1770, finished 1773, rebuilt after earthquakes in 1802 (under Captain Simon Tamondong)
- **FamilySearch Collection #1646454:** Philippines, Lingayen-Dagupan Catholic Archdiocese Parish Registers, 1615-1982 — should include St. Dominic parish

### Angel B. Fernandez Made San Carlos a City
- **Republic Act No. 4487** — signed by President Marcos, ratified by the people
- **Effective:** January 1, 1966
- **Sponsor:** Congressman Angel B. Fernandez (2nd District of Pangasinan)
- **Connection:** Angel B. Fernandez is the FERCOLLA clan patriarch from Dagupan who was first mayor of Dagupan, then congressman for the 2nd District (which includes San Carlos)
- **Significance:** The FERCOLLA Fernandez dynasty from Dagupan had direct political authority over San Carlos City — they represented it in Congress. This strengthens the possible link between Bienvenido Fernandez and the broader Fernandez political network.

### San Carlos City Historical Context
- **Original name:** Binalatongan — capital of the ancient Kingdom of Caboloan (Luyag na Caboloan)
- **Founded:** 1587 by Dominican missionaries (Fr. Francisco dela Rama)
- **Kingdom encompassed:** Tarlac, Zambales, Nueva Ecija, La Union, Pangasinan, Benguet
- **Key revolts:** Andres Malong (1660), Juan de la Cruz Palaris (1762-64)
- **Name change:** King Carlos III of Spain ordered it renamed "San Carlos" as punishment for the Palaris revolt
- **City charter:** 1966 (RA 4487)
- **Population:** 208,330 (2024 census) — most populous LGU in Pangasinan
- **86 barangays** including Balaya, Ano, San Juan (site of Binalatongan ruins)
- **Notable native:** Fernando Poe Sr. (born 1916, San Carlos City) — father of FPJ, "Da King"
