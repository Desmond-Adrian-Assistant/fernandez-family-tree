### 1. Francisco Sabangan Abalos — Full Obituary (Arizona Republic, March 2008)

**A San Carlos-born Abalos who became a legendary Filipino-American!**

- **Full name:** Francisco Sabangan Abalos
- **Born:** June 4, 1929, San Carlos, Pangasinan, Philippines
- **Died:** March 10, 2008, Peoria, Arizona
- **Wife:** Cecille (Abalos)
- **Children:** Cherylene, Robert, Ronald, Richard, and Francisco Jr.
- **Daughter-in-laws:** Carmen, Mary, Veronica
- **Grandchildren:** Leilani, Lila, Steven, Nicholas, Abigail, Alianna (6 total)

**Remarkable Career:**
- Entered US Navy in 1957 at age 30
- Became "man of the month" for exceeding 100th month of sea duty as a submariner
- Climbed to highest position of non-commissioned Naval Officer (Chief Petty Officer)
- Served in Vietnam War
- Retired as **Chief of the US Navy** (highest NCO rank)
- In 1969, became **first Filipino ever to hold a Builder-Broker license in California**
- Became **first Filipino Investment Counselor and Consultant**
- In 1997, picked as one of the **top 20 most outstanding Filipinos in the US and Canada**

**Burial:** Resthaven Park Cemetery, Peoria/Phoenix, AZ
**Published:** Arizona Republic, March 13, 2008

### Analysis: Is This Aurea's Brother?

**Middle name "Sabangan" = mother's maiden name** in Filipino convention. If Aurea's mother is Filomena **Rosario**, then Francisco Sabangan Abalos's mother was "Sabangan" — NOT Rosario.

**HOWEVER** — this could still be significant:
- Hilario Abalos (Aurea's father) could have been married twice, or Francisco could be from a different Hilario
- There could be multiple Abalos families in San Carlos with children named Francisco
- The Ancestry record #3316288 (Francisco Abalos, child of Hilario + Filomena Rosario) may be a DIFFERENT Francisco Abalos than this one

**Verdict:** Francisco Sabangan Abalos is from a **different Abalos family branch** in San Carlos. His mother's maiden name was Sabangan (not Rosario). BUT this confirms that the Abalos surname has deep roots in San Carlos City specifically, with multiple Abalos families producing successful emigrants.
