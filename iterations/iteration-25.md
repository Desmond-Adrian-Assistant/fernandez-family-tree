### 1. 🔥 Laoac is 89% Ilocano (NOT Native Pangasinan)
From the official municipal history:
> "Ethnologically, the present population is predominantly of Ilocano stock, with around 89% of the original residents coming from the Ilocos Region, notably Ilocos Sur, La Union, Ilocos Norte and Abra."

This is critical context: The Tamondong and Abalos families in Laoac are **Ilocano settlers**, not native Pangasinan-speakers. This aligns with:
- Eduardo **Corpuz** Tamondong in San Carlos (Corpuz = Ilocano surname)

### 8. Laoac Etymology & Origins
- Name from Ilocano "Nag-la-oa daytoy nga tay-aken" = "How wide this plain is!"
- First Ilocano settlers arrived in the **late 1800s**
- Laoac was the **biggest barangay of Manaoag** before separation
- Separation movement started early 1900s by Don Vicente Gavino and Don Jacinto Nuñez
- First mayor (Westrimundo Tabayoyong) was **assassinated** Sept 6, 1985 at the municipal plaza
- Barangay names reflect the migration origins: Casanestebanan (San Esteban), Casantiagoan (Santiago), Casampagaan (San Paga)

### 9. Redistricting: Laoac Now in 5th District (Not 4th)
- Wikipedia confirms Laoac is in the **5th congressional district** (Rep. Ramon V. Guico III)
- Previously discussed as if in the 4th District — needs correction
- The 4th District includes San Carlos City and Dagupan; the 5th includes Manaoag, Laoac, and eastern municipalities
- This means the 2022 Tamondong/Abalos/Rosario Board Member race was in 4th District (San Carlos area), while Laoac is 5th

### 10. Laoac Barangay Names = Ilocano Settlement Pattern
All 22 barangays, many with "Cas-" prefix indicating "lugar ng" (place of) + saint name:
- Casanestebanan (San Esteban) → settlers from San Esteban, Ilocos Sur
- Casantiagoan (Santiago) → settlers from Santiago
- Casampagaan → place of Sampaga (jasmine)
- Caaringayan, Cabilaoan, Cabulalaan, Calaoagan, Calmay → all Ilocano place-name formations
- This naming convention confirms mass Ilocano migration — settlers named barangays after their home municipalities
