## Mariano Fernandez WWII — New Corroboration

### Bamban WWII Museum (March 2025 article — 80th Anniversary of Liberation)
**CONFIRMED: TMA Squadrons 401+402 covered Sta. Ignacia–Gerona zone**

From bambanmuseum.org published March 20, 2025:
> "The Tarlaqueno USAFFE guerrillas were members of the Tarlac Military Area, under the command of Captain Albert A. Hendrickson, and operating the area of Sta. Ignacia – Gerona which were covered by the sphere of combat operations of the Flying Squadron (Squadron 401 and 402)."

The article shows a WWII photograph of guerrillas presenting arms to the first American troops entering Sta. Ignacia, identified as members of TMA LGAF. Confirms previous iteration 57 findings.

Key notes:
- Photo taken mid-January 1945, likely by members of 40th Division U.S. Army
- The article cites NARA source: "HQ Tarlac Military Area TMA Luzon Guerrilla Army Forces LGAF, US National Archives NARA, College Park, MD."
- Also present: Aglipay Battalion, General Antonio Luna Regiment under Col. Eusebio Aquino's 3rd Regional Command, HUKBALAHAP
- Guerrillas were armed with Enfield, M1 Carbine, and M1 Garand — civilian clothing

**Significance:** Mariano Fernandez (b. ~1905, settled in Danzo/Gerona by 1930s) would have been ~36-39 years old during the 1942-1945 occupation and liberation. He lived in the exact geographic zone covered by TMA Squadrons 401 and 402. His guerrilla service is very plausible. NARA file 102-22 (TMA LGAF) should contain his name if he was registered.
