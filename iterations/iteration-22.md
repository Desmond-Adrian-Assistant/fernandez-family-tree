### 3. Clark Air Base — Immigration Pathway Context (Expanded)
- **At peak (~1990)**: 70,000+ Filipino workers directly employed or benefiting from base operations
- **$220 million annually** injected into local economy
- **June 10, 1991**: Operation Fiery Vigil — emergency evacuation of ~20,000 people (US personnel + dependents)
- **Mt. Pinatubo eruption June 15, 1991** devastated Clark Air Base
- **Philippine Senate rejected treaty renewal** September 16, 1991
- **November 26, 1991**: Base officially turned over to Philippines
- The closure displaced tens of thousands of Filipino workers and their families
- Many Clark workers had connections to US military personnel and could petition for immigration through:
  - Family reunification (relatives already in US)
  - Employment-based visas (skills acquired at Clark)
  - Special Immigrant status (INA 101(a)(27)(K)) for Filipino military enlistees
- **This is a strong candidate for the Cervantes family's immigration pathway**: Angeles City → Clark employment → US immigration (post-1991 closure or earlier through military connections)
