### Full Danzo Barangay History (Official Municipal Source — geronatarlac.gov.ph)

**Origin story confirmed and expanded:**
> "Many years ago, long before the American Occupation, Danzo was a thick forest. Tall talahib grasses covered the whole area. At night nothing was heard except the hooting of the owls."

> "A version about the origin of the name Danzo is that it is a derivation from the Ransouan, the **Pangasinan name** for a hiding or resting place."

> "Groups of a bad man called 'Tulisan' hid in this barrio and its vicinity. They held up and grabbed the money and other valuables of the people who passed the place."

> "This group came from **Pangasinan**. They did not stay long for they moved to another place again."

> "Later during the Spanish regime, an old man from a nearby barrio named **Ignacio Arciaga** with his family bravely came to settle in this haunted place."

### Pinasling Barangay — Another Pangasinan Connection

Neighboring barangay Pinasling (east of Danzo) has a revealing history:
> "The word Pinasling is the **family name of a certain Pangasinan man** who was an intruder in the sparse population portion of Barog."
> "This certain Pangasinan was driven away by the former inhabitants."
> "The inhabitants grouped themselves in what is called Pinasling and named the place in memory of the Pangasinan who was driven away."

**Founders of Pinasling were from San Nicolas, Ilocos Norte** — same town where Edilberto A. Tamondong had his Masonic Lodge (No. 217). San Nicolas also borders San Carlos City.

### Key Discovery: Mariano Benitez = First Teniente of Danzo

The first teniente del barrio of Danzo was **Mariano Benitez**. The Benitez surname appears in BOTH:
- **San Carlos**: Judge Juan Benitez (hid Col. Ramsey, WWII guerrilla leader)
- **Gerona/Danzo**: Mariano Benitez (first barrio leader)

This is NOT coincidental — Danzo was founded by Pangasinan settlers, and the Benitez principalía family operates across both territories. The fact that a Benitez was the FIRST teniente and a Fernandez served as FOURTH teniente suggests the Fernandez family arrived shortly after the Benitez-led founding of Danzo.

### USAFFE 21st Division Retreat Through Gerona

Barangay Calayaan documents that "the retreat of the USAFFE particularly the 23rd Regiment, 21st Division under the command of Col. Liberato Littawa took place from the Poblacion to this barrio." This confirms Gerona was a WWII combat zone — Mariano Fernandez (b. ~1905) would have experienced this directly.
