## Key Findings

### 🔥 Carlos Cervantes' Family — ORIGINALLY FROM LUZON
- **Carlos Cervantes** married **Carlota de los Santos** — had **16 children**
- Carlos died September 7, 1933 in Davao
- **"His forebears were originally from Luzon"** — migrated to Cateel (Davao Oriental) first → then Caraga (Davao Oriental) → then old town of Davao
- The specific Luzon province is NOT stated — could be Pangasinan, Pampanga, Ilocos, or anywhere in Luzon
- Carlos owned significant property in Barrio Obrero, Davao — **Cervantes Street** named after him
- Supreme Court case G.R. No. L-47342 (1941): Carlos's estate contested a loan/sale document
- The Luzon-to-Mindanao migration pattern is well-documented for Ilocano and Pangasinan families in the late 1800s

### Cenon Cervantes — Detailed Career Path (PNB Postings)
- **1918:** Started as lowly PNB employee in Davao City
- **1930s:** Promoted to PNB Branch Manager in **Dagupan** — this is when he was initiated into Pangasinan Lodge No. 56 (1933)
- **WWII:** PNB Manager in Iloilo — hid P1,729,000 from Japanese, joined resistance
- **Post-war:** Chairman/GM of National Abaca Corp (Roxas appointment)
- **Quirino era:** Technical Assistant to PNB President
- **1952-1958:** Managing sugar-sales operation in **New York City**
- **1958-1960:** Manager of sugar centrals in **Pampanga and Negros Occidental**
- **1960-1969:** Comptroller-Treasurer of Trans-Philippines Investment Corporation
- **Died:** December 1, 1978 (age 75)
- **Masonic:** Initiated Pangasinan Lodge No. 56 (1933), Master (1938), Grand Master (1951), SGIG Scottish Rite (1965)
- Also held memberships in New York Masonic bodies (Phoenix Chapter No. 2, Constantine Commandery No. 48)
- **Installed by MW Conrado BENITEZ** both as Lodge Master (1938) and Grand Master (1951)

## Analysis & Implications

### The Luzon Origin Question
Carlos Cervantes' family "originally from Luzon" who migrated to Mindanao follows a pattern common among:
- **Ilocano settlers** (the largest group migrating to Mindanao in late 1800s-early 1900s)
- **Pangasinan settlers** (also significant Mindanao migration)
- **Pampanga families** were less likely to migrate to Mindanao (already in a rich agricultural province)

The fact that Cenon was posted to **Dagupan** (Pangasinan) by PNB in the 1930s and joined Pangasinan Lodge No. 56 there could suggest a pre-existing family connection to the region, or it could be pure coincidence of PNB posting. The article explicitly says "forebears originally from Luzon" without specifying which province.

### Cervantes-Pampanga Connection Assessment
- **CONFIRMED:** Cenon Cervantes managed sugar centrals in Pampanga (1958-60)
- **BUT:** This was a 2-year corporate posting, not a family settlement
- **NO evidence** of Cervantes children settling in Pampanga/Angeles City
- **NO evidence** connecting this Cervantes branch to Janice's Diosdado Cervantes
- The Pampanga sugar connection is geographic overlap, not genealogical proof
- The DL Cervantes Construction link was already ruled out (wife is Cynthia M., not Natividad Cabrera)

### Probability Assessment: Is Janice's Cervantes family connected to Cenon's?
**LOW probability** for the following reasons:
1. Cenon's family is from **Davao** (originally Luzon → Mindanao migration)
2. Janice's family is from **Angeles City, Pampanga** — a completely different region
3. Cervantes has 33,562 bearers in Philippines — it's a moderately common surname
4. Cenon's Pampanga stay was temporary (2 years) and work-related
5. No Cervantes children appear to have settled in Pampanga
6. Behn (the most documented child) had no children of his own
7. The 8 private Geni profiles could include someone in Pampanga, but this is speculation

**HOWEVER:** The "originally from Luzon" origin of Carlos Cervantes leaves open the slim possibility that the broader Cervantes clan had branches that remained in Luzon — one of which could have ended up in Pampanga. This would require physical records (baptismal/civil) to prove or disprove.
