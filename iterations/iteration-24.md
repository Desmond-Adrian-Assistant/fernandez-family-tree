### 🔥 MAJOR: Pangasinan Masonic Network Connects ALL Family Surnames

**Three key Masonic lodges in Pangasinan form the principalía's fraternal backbone:**

#### 1. Pangasinan Lodge No. 56 (Dagupan City, est. 1921)
- **Founded by:** Senator Daniel Maramba, Senator Pedro Maria Sison, Chief Justice Manuel Moran — Dagupan political elite
- **Fernandez members:**
  - **WB Guillermo Fernandez** — Past Master, proposed temple relocation in 1995
  - **Bro. Alipio F. Fernandez Jr.** — FERCOLLA Mayor of Dagupan, donated land for temple construction
  - **Johnson Fernandez** — Masonic District RI-A officer (2015)
  - **Francisco Fernandez** — Worshipful Master (2024 leadership directory, Scribd)
- **🔥 Cenon S. Cervantes** — Grand Master of Philippine Freemasonry (1951), Master of this lodge in 1938

#### 2. Edilberto A. Tamondong Sr. Memorial Lodge No. 217 (San Nicolas, est. 1977)
- **Originally named:** "Red Arrow Division Lodge No. 217" — after the 32nd Infantry Division that liberated San Nicolas from the Japanese
- **Renamed 1989** to honor Edilberto A. Tamondong Sr. (1908-1989), who donated the lodge lot on Osmena St., San Nicolas
- **Created from:** Agno Lodge No. 75 members in San Nicolas, Natividad, and San Quintin (seceded in 1977 after officer election controversy)
- **Constituted:** June 26, 1977 — Edilberto Tamondong himself was a "Masonic dignitary" at the ceremony
- **Location:** San Nicolas, Pangasinan — **directly borders San Carlos City** (Adrian's family's base)
- **WWII connection:** Red Arrow Division = US 32nd Infantry Division that fought in Pangasinan during liberation

#### 3. Agno Lodge No. 75 (originally Rosales, now Tayug, est. 1921)
- Named after the Agno River (flows through San Carlos)
- Parent lodge of the Tamondong Lodge (members seceded in 1977)
- Operated in eastern Pangasinan — San Carlos, Tayug, Rosales territory
- WWII: Went dark during Japanese occupation, rebuilt 1945

### 🔥🔥 CENON DE LOS SANTOS CERVANTES — Grand Master of Philippine Freemasonry (1951)

**Full biography from Grand Lodge of the Philippines:**
- **Born:** March 29, 1903, Davao City
- **Died:** December 1, 1978 (age 75)
- **Parents:** Carlos Cervantes & Carlota de los Santos — **16 children**
- **Wife:** Rosario E. Holcomb (of Zamboanga City) — **11 children**, including:
  - **Benjamin Roberto "Behn" Holcombe Cervantes** (1938-2013) — famous film director, theater pioneer, anti-Marcos activist, on the Bantayog ng mga Bayani Wall of Remembrance
  - **Tiburcio Cervantes** — Venerable Master, Lodge of Perfection, Davao Bodies (Scottish Rite)
- **Career:** Philippine National Bank (PNB) — started as lowly employee in Davao (1918), rose to PNB Branch Manager of **Dagupan** (1930s), then assigned to Lucena, Cabanatuan, Cebu, Iloilo
- **WWII heroism:** While PNB Manager at Iloilo, hid P1,729,000 in bank funds from the Japanese, joined resistance as undercover agent for Col. Macario Peralta's guerrillas. Returned every centavo after liberation.
- **Post-war:** Chairman/General Manager of National Abaca and Other Fibers Corporation (appointed by President Roxas), sugar sales in New York City (1952-58), sugar centrals manager in **Pampanga** and Negros Occidental (1958-60)
- **Masonic career:** Initiated 1933 in Pangasinan Lodge No. 56 → Master 1938 → Junior Grand Warden 1948 → Senior Grand Warden 1949 → Deputy Grand Master 1950 → **Grand Master 1951** → Scottish Rite SGIG 1965

**🔥 Significance for family research:**
1. **A CERVANTES who was the most prominent Filipino Mason of his era, based in Dagupan** — the exact city where Adrian's paternal family has deep roots
2. The Cervantes surname is from **Adrian's MATERNAL side** (Janice Cervantes married Raul Fernandez) — but Janice's Cervantes family is from **Pampanga**, not Pangasinan
3. **HOWEVER:** Cenon Cervantes managed sugar centrals in **PAMPANGA** (1958-60) — geographical overlap with the Angeles City Cervantes family
4. Cenon was born in Davao but his family had 16 children — branches likely spread across the Philippines
5. **Conrado Benitez** installed Cenon as both Lodge Master and Grand Master — BENITEZ is also a San Carlos principalía surname (Judge Juan Benitez hid Col. Ramsey during WWII)
6. At minimum, this shows the Cervantes surname was embedded in the **same Pangasinan Masonic elite** as the Fernandez family
