## 🔥 Claro "Lito" Cabrera — Martyr from Sapang Bato, Angeles City (Bantayog ng mga Bayani)

**Source:** bantayogngmgabayani.org — official Bantayog ng mga Bayani (Wall of Remembrance) honoree listing

### Profile
- **Name:** Claro "Lito" Cabrera
- **Community:** Sapang Bato, Angeles City, Pampanga — on the edge of Clark Air Base
- **Background:** Working-class family, 8 siblings total, mother separated from father
- **Quit school after 2 years of high school** — typical of economically marginal families near Clark
- **Early 1980s:** Joined Concerned Citizens of Pampanga — "oldest cause-oriented organization in Central Luzon"
- **Activities:** Rallies, anti-Marcos organizing, working with youth, teaching handicrafts, making hand-printed shirts
- **Fate:** Picked up by constabulary soldiers May 28, 1984. Body found dumped on Apalit River bank — multiple stab wounds. Age at death: approx. 28.

### Geographic Significance
- **Sapang Bato is a barangay of Angeles City** (distinct from San Nicolás/Clark proper)
- Located on the western edge of the city — literally adjacent to Clark Air Base perimeter
- Clark employed tens of thousands of Filipinos; the adjacent barangays were economically dependent on base activity
- The Cabrera family lived in this specific zone, suggesting they were among the families that settled near Clark (common among workers' families)

### Connection Assessment
- **Name match:** Claro Cabrera's surname = Natividad CABRERA Cervantes (Janice's mother's maiden name)
- **Location match:** Sapang Bato, Angeles City — consistent with Janice's family being from Angeles City
- **Timeline:** Born ~1956-57 (age ~28 in 1984). Siblings' generation matches Natividad Cabrera (~born 1930s-1940s?)
- **Social class:** Working class (construction, informal economy near Clark Base) — consistent with the non-elite profile of Cervantes/Cabrera families confirmed in prior research
- **Confidence:** LOW-MEDIUM — Cabrera is common (69K bearers in Philippines); Lito Cabrera is from the right area but no direct family link established

### Key Detail: "Concerned Citizens of Pampanga" = Anti-Marcos Activism
- This places the Angeles City Cabrera family in the progressive/activist milieu of Central Luzon in the early 1980s
- Importantly: Janice Cervantes emigrated to the US — this was the SAME era (1981: Bienvenido's family arrived in Chicago; 1984: Marcos martial law crackdowns intensifying)
- Clark Base workers had easier access to US immigration pathways

## Analysis: Kapampangan Context for Cervantes + Cabrera

### The Clark Air Base Economy — Key to Understanding Both Surnames
- Clark Air Base (1903-1991) was the primary employer in the Angeles City economy
- **70,000+ Filipino workers** directly employed or economically dependent
- Families near Clark (Sapang Bato, Malabanias, Cutcut, Mining barangays) had complex social stratification:
  - **Higher level:** Civil servants, teachers, professionals (Clark employees)
  - **Middle level:** Artisans, shopkeepers, service workers
  - **Marginal:** Day laborers, informal workers, hospitality industry

### Why Both Surnames Are Rare in Pampanga
- Cervantes (33,562 Philippine bearers) and Cabrera (69,376 bearers) are NOT concentrated in Pampanga
- Pampanga's dominant surnames are indigenous Kapampangan names (Mallari, Dayrit, Canlas) mixed with Spanish
- Cervantes and Cabrera are pan-Philippine Spanish surnames, NOT specifically Kapampangan
- This suggests both families may have migrated TO Angeles City from other provinces (not native Pampanga families)
- OR they were among the early Spanish-period colonizers who adopted these surnames before 1849

### Possible Migration Origin of Cervantes Family
Given the absence of Cervantes from top Pampanga surnames, the Diosdados Cervantes family may have:
1. **Migrated from another province** (Manila, Bulacan, or even Pangasinan) to Angeles City for work
2. **Been part of the US-era immigration** to the Clark area (1903+) when American military brought economic activity
3. **Indigenous Pampanga family** that adopted Cervantes surname in 1849 (possible but surname not in Catálogo for Pampanga)
