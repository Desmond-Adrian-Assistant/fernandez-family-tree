## Key Findings

### 🔥 Umingan Founded by Settlers from MANGALDAN
From the pangasinanhistory.weebly.com primary source:
> "The first settlers were the Umingan family who came from the coastal town of **Mangaldan**."

This is the same Mangaldan that is the **Abalos political stronghold** (Ritchie G., Rolly G., Florida, Maria Teresa Abalos — all documented in Iteration 14). The migration path:
- **Mangaldan** (coastal, Abalos stronghold) → **Umingan** (inland, Barangay Don Justo Abalos)
- Umingan is 97% Ilocano-descended, with 3% from "coastal towns of Pangasinan"
- The Abalos family likely came from the Mangaldan settler wave

### 🔥 TWO Fernandez Councilors in Umingan Municipality
- **Fred A. Fernandez** — Councilor (2022-2025 term)
- **Siann Liandro M. Fernandez** — Councilor (2025 term, per Wikipedia update)

Umingan is in the **6th Congressional District** (different from San Carlos/Dagupan's 4th District), but the presence of:
- Barangay Don Justo Abalos (Abalos principalía)
- TWO Fernandez councilors
- Mangaldan settler origins (Abalos stronghold)
...in a single municipality is another data point in the Fernandez-Abalos network across eastern Pangasinan.

### Abalos Surname Global Distribution (Forebears.io)
- **47,516 worldwide** (11,828th most common globally)
- **31,719 in Philippines** (1 in 3,192 people; rank 233rd most common)
- **29% live in Ilocos Region** (includes Pangasinan)
- **15% in Central Luzon**, **14% in NCR**
- **Spanish-Sephardic origin** — from the village of Ábalos in La Rioja, Spain
- Listed in "Sangre Judia" (Sephardic surname origins) as a habitual name
- The village of Ábalos appears in the 740 AD Albeldense Chronicle (Alfonso I expedition along the Ebro River)

### Umingan Barangay Naming Pattern — "Don" Prefix
Two adjacent barangays in Umingan use the "Don" prefix:
- **Don Justo Abalos** (formerly Caroan) — named after an Abalos principalía leader
- **Don Montano** (formerly Cadamortisan) — named after another local patron

These barangays are adjacent to each other and to Maseil-seil. The "Don" honorific confirms both namesakes were of principalía (landed gentry) status.

### Umingan Historical Context
- **Founded:** May 5, 1843 (per Wikipedia); local historians say "established in the 1880s"
- Originally part of Nueva Ecija (all towns east of Agno River belonged to NE)
- Later incorporated into Pangasinan
- 97% Ilocano-descended settlers
- At foot of Caraballo Mountains — eastern frontier of Pangasinan
- Named after the "Umingan family" from Mangaldan — a bearded patriarch who generously fed travelers
